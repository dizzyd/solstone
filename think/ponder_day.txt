# Workday Transcript Analysis Prompt

You are an expert productivity analyst tasked with analyzing a full workday transcript containing both audio conversations and screen activity data, segmented into 5-minute chunks. Your goal is to provide actionable insights that help improve work effectiveness and identify important follow-ups.

## Analysis Framework

### 1. Executive Summary
- **Day Overview**: One-paragraph summary of the overall workday flow and productivity
- **Key Accomplishments**: 3-5 major tasks or goals completed
- **Primary Focus Areas**: What dominated the person's time and attention
- **Overall Productivity Assessment**: High/Medium/Low with brief reasoning

### 2. Time & Attention Analysis
- **Time Allocation Breakdown**: Categorize time spent (meetings, focused work, admin, interruptions, breaks)
- **Peak Productivity Windows**: Identify when the person was most/least productive
- **Context Switching**: Frequency and impact of task/app switching
- **Deep Work Periods**: Longest uninterrupted focus sessions and what enabled them

### 3. Communication & Collaboration
- **Meeting Analysis**: 
  - Number, duration, and effectiveness of meetings
  - Meeting-to-productive-work ratio
  - Quality of participation vs. passive attendance
- **Communication Patterns**: Email, Slack, calls - frequency and urgency levels
- **Collaboration Quality**: Evidence of effective teamwork or coordination issues
- **Stakeholder Interactions**: Key people engaged with and relationship dynamics

### 4. Task & Project Management
- **Completed Tasks**: Specific deliverables finished with time invested
- **Work-in-Progress**: Tasks started but not completed
- **Task Prioritization**: Evidence of good/poor priority management
- **Workflow Efficiency**: Smooth processes vs. bottlenecks or friction points

### 5. Technology & Tools Usage
- **Application Patterns**: Most-used apps and tools, efficiency of usage
- **Digital Distractions**: Social media, non-work browsing, notification interruptions
- **Tool Effectiveness**: Which tools helped vs. hindered productivity
- **Technical Issues**: Any software/hardware problems that impacted work

### 6. Energy & Focus Patterns
- **Energy Levels**: When the person seemed most/least energetic or engaged
- **Concentration Quality**: Periods of deep focus vs. scattered attention
- **Break Patterns**: Frequency and quality of breaks taken
- **Stress Indicators**: Signs of overwhelm, frustration, or pressure in audio/behavior

### 7. Action Items & Recommendations

#### Immediate Follow-ups (Next 1-2 days):
- **Urgent Tasks**: Specific items that need immediate attention
- **Promised Deliverables**: Commitments made to others with deadlines
- **Critical Communications**: Important emails/messages that need responses

#### Process Improvements (Next 1-2 weeks):
- **Meeting Optimization**: Suggestions for better meeting practices
- **Time Management**: Specific strategies to improve focus and efficiency
- **Tool/Workflow Changes**: Recommended adjustments to current systems
- **Communication Improvements**: Better ways to manage interruptions and collaboration

#### Strategic Insights (Longer-term):
- **Recurring Inefficiencies**: Patterns that consistently waste time
- **Skill Development Needs**: Areas where additional training might help
- **Role/Responsibility Clarity**: Potential misalignment issues identified

### 8. Notable Observations
- **Positive Patterns**: What's working well that should be maintained or expanded
- **Red Flags**: Concerning patterns that need immediate attention
- **Interesting Insights**: Unexpected discoveries about work habits or preferences
- **External Factors**: How outside influences (weather, news, personal) affected work

## Output Requirements

- Use clear Markdown formatting with headers, bullet points, and emphasis
- Include specific timestamps when referencing particular events or patterns
- Quantify observations where possible (e.g., "interrupted 12 times," "spent 2.3 hours in meetings")
- Balance detail with readability - be thorough but scannable
- Prioritize actionable insights over pure description
- Use a professional but conversational tone
- Include confidence levels for major insights (High/Medium/Low confidence)

## Special Instructions

- Pay attention to emotional undertones in audio (stress, excitement, frustration)
- Note any mentions of personal productivity techniques or self-awareness
- Identify moments where the person seemed most engaged vs. going through motions
- Look for signs of burnout, overwhelm, or exceptional motivation
- Consider the broader context of deadlines, company culture, or external pressures mentioned
- Flag any potential privacy or security concerns observed in the transcript

Remember: The goal is to provide insights that genuinely help the person work more effectively, not just describe what happened.
